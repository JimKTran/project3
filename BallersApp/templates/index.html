NBA Historical Data Analysis and Visualisations

Introduction

        In 1891 basketball was invented by James Naismith and since then its popularity has increased significantly. Basketball is played all over the world and is at the forefront of the American sporting scene. The massive growth created a culture among many countries, kick starting NBA data collection. Today this data is used to pick the best players among many, predict future great players and determine individual player’s strength and weaknesses. Our team has carefully sorted, selected and assembled numerous data sets to create interesting fan-based visualizations.

Background

        This is the third part of a larger NBA statistical analysis report. Data has been collected from multiple trusted data sources and has gone through a series of data cleansing procedures. There have also been numerous data transformations, in order to present the best results. Graphs on this page are meant to be interactive to give the best user experience.

Findings

  • The first dominant player is found in the 60’s with more championship wins than any other player. It is very unlikely that another player will ever dominate in this way.

  • To be considered one of the greatest players of all time, players should have at least 7000 playoffs minutes (unless your Larry Bird).

  • The Centre position dominated the league until the mid-80’s and has recently seen a resurgence.

  • There is a significant drop in efficiency for all positions which is seen in 2 separate years between the 1980's and 2015.

  • Comparing total points of a team to their win total, we see that a few teams rely heavily on their defense to win.

  • The highest concentration of successful shots is made within 4 feet of the net and beyond the arch.

  • Players in the 1960’s and earlier have no data collected for steals in their career. This category was not created until 1973.
